1.3 The Davenport Constant

Definition 1.3.1 Zero-Sum Free

ZeroSumFree A multiset \(s\) over an additive group is zero-sum free if no nonempty submultiset of \(s\) sums to zero.

Theorem 1.3.2 Sum of Replicate

multiset_sum_replicate The sum of \(k\) copies of \(a\) equals \(k \cdot a\) (i.e., \(k \smul a\)).

Theorem 1.3.3 Lower Bound: \(\Dav (\ZZ /n\ZZ ) \geq n\)

zeroSumFree_replicate_one def:zero_sum_free, thm:sum_replicate For \(n {\gt} 1\), the multiset of \((n-1)\) copies of \(1\) in \(\ZZ /n\ZZ \) is zero-sum free. Therefore \(\Dav (\ZZ /n\ZZ ) \geq n\).

Proof ▼

Any nonempty submultiset consists of \(k\) copies of \(1\) for \(1 \leq k \leq n-1\). Its sum is \(k \in \ZZ /n\ZZ \). Since \(1 \leq k \leq n-1\), we have \(n \nmid k\), so \(k \neq 0\) in \(\ZZ /n\ZZ \).

Key insight: The extremal instance (longest zero-sum free multiset) is maximally structured—it consists entirely of copies of a single element. Hard instances are not random; they are rigid.

Theorem 1.3.4 Upper Bound: \(\Dav (\ZZ /n\ZZ ) \leq n\)

davenport_upper def:zero_sum_free For \(n {\gt} 0\), every multiset of \(n\) elements in \(\ZZ /n\ZZ \) contains a nonempty submultiset summing to zero.

Proof sketch ▼

Let \(a_1, \ldots , a_n \in \ZZ /n\ZZ \). Consider the prefix sums \(s_0 = 0, s_1 = a_1, s_2 = a_1 + a_2, \ldots , s_n = a_1 + \cdots + a_n\). These are \(n+1\) elements of \(\ZZ /n\ZZ \), which has \(n\) elements. By the pigeonhole principle, \(s_i = s_j\) for some \(i {\lt} j\). Then \(a_{i+1} + \cdots + a_j = s_j - s_i = 0\).

Theorem 1.3.5 \(\Dav (\ZZ /n\ZZ ) = n\)

davenport_ZMod thm:davenport_lower, thm:davenport_upper For \(n {\gt} 1\):

  1. Every multiset of \(n\) elements in \(\ZZ /n\ZZ \) has a nonempty zero-sum submultiset.

  2. There exists a multiset of \(n-1\) elements in \(\ZZ /n\ZZ \) with no nonempty zero-sum submultiset.
