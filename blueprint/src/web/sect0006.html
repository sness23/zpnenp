2.1 Inverse Zero-Sum Theorems

Theorem 2.1.1 Inverse Davenport for \(\ZZ /n\ZZ \)

def:zero_sum_free The zero-sum free multisets of maximal length \(n-1\) in \(\ZZ /n\ZZ \) are precisely those consisting of \(n-1\) copies of a single element \(g\) with \(\gcd (g, n) = 1\).

Theorem 2.1.2 Inverse EGZ

thm:davenport_zmod The multisets of length \(2n-2\) in \(\ZZ /n\ZZ \) that do not contain an \(n\)-element zero-sum submultiset are precisely those consisting of \((n-1)\) copies of element \(a\) and \((n-1)\) copies of element \(b\), with \(\gcd (a - b, n) = 1\).
